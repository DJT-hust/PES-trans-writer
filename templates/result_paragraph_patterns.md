# Result Paragraph Patterns

## Comparison paragraph
Compared with `<baseline>`, `<method>` reduces `<metric>` from `<a>` to `<b>`, corresponding to `<x>%`. The reduction is mainly caused by `<mechanism>`, which `<technical explanation>`. This indicates that `<method>` is most beneficial when `<condition>`.

## Ablation paragraph
Removing `<component>` increases `<metric>` by `<x>%` and changes `<secondary metric>` by `<y>%`. The change confirms that `<component>` contributes to `<mechanism>`, rather than only improving the numerical conditioning of the optimization model.

## Sensitivity paragraph
As `<parameter>` increases from `<a>` to `<b>`, `<metric>` changes monotonically/non-monotonically from `<x>` to `<y>`. The trend is explained by `<constraint/price/network effect>`. Beyond `<threshold>`, the marginal benefit decreases because `<binding constraint>` becomes active.
